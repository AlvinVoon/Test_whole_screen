Ensuring Road Safety: Types of Road Signs You Should Know About

Written by BC • Updated on 21 June 2023

As we traverse the vibrant roads of Malaysia, we become part of a diverse and bustling transportation system. However, amidst the beauty and excitement, road safety remains of paramount importance. Malaysia's comprehensive array of traffic signs serves as a vital tool in ensuring the safety of drivers, pedestrians, and cyclists. In this post, we will explore the correlation between road safety and Malaysia's distinct categories of traffic signs: warning signs, mandatory signs, priority signs, prohibitory signs, and information signs. Join us as we unravel the significance of these signs in fostering a culture of road safety across the nation.

#1 Warning Signs

The warning signs are placed to warn road users of a potentially dangerous traffic situation. Most of the signs have diamond shapes and yellow background, contrasting with their black pictograms. You can see them alongside the road, preparing drivers for what lies ahead.

Image Source: Carsome

#2 Prohibition Signs

Prohibited signs are usually in red colour with a white background. Sometimes, these road signs can be in yellow or blue backgrounds. The prohibition signs forbid a certain action. Drivers are required to obey these types of road signs.

Image Source: Wikipedia

#3 Mandatory Signs

Mandatory signs come in blue background with white pictograms. These signs are placed at locations where drivers are required to adhere.

Image Source: Wikipedia

#4 Information Signs

Information Signs are blue backgrounds and white pictograms, while those with green backgrounds and white pictograms are often located on the highway. You can spot the signs almost everywhere as they provide only general information or extra information about the road.

Image Source: Wikipedia

#5 Speed Limit Signs

Speed Limit Signs vary in colour such as red, white, yellow and orange. Drivers are required to follow as it shows the particular speed limit for the road. The speed limits are to prevent potential accidents or mishaps from occuring. If a driver goes over the speed limit, it might cause the car to skid. If drivers fail to follow the speed limit, they will be at risk of getting fined when caught by the poilice.

Image Source: fatberry

As we conclude our exploration of Malaysia's traffic signs through the lens of road safety, it becomes evident that these signs are more than just symbols on the roadside. They serve as indispensable tools in guiding and protecting road users, enhancing our overall safety and well-being. By understanding the significance of warning signs, adhering to mandatory signs, respecting priority signs, complying with prohibitory signs, and leveraging the information provided by informational signs, we collectively contribute to fostering a culture of road safety. Let us embrace the responsibility of being mindful road users, ensuring that every journey in Malaysia is secure for all.
